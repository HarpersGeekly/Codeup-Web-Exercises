Ridley Scott

I love different themes, different venues, different movies. I love to jump about and tackle different subjects. I have no intellectual master plan.

Early Life:

  • November 30, 1937: Born in South Shields, North East England.
  • 1954 - 1958: Studied Design at West Hartlepool College of Art.
  • 1958 - 1961: Attended Royal College of Art in London.
  • Throughout the 1960's he was a television and commercial director.
I realised very early the need to learn fast and absorb all that I could and then make my own decisions. I had strong views even then and could sort the wheat from the chaff, and that’s still important today in everything I do.
[Recalling an early set visit to Pinewood Studios for Dr. No] The first thing I saw was Sean Connery, dressed in a tuxedo, just standing there waiting and smoking a cigarette, while they were fiddling with the camera. Terence Young, the director, was dressed in a navy blue, very smart blazer with gold buttons, polished shoes, a button-down shirt, hair plastered down and he was shouting at the top of his voice to get the whole thing moving. And it all looked so extraordinarily difficult. And that was it for me, that's what I wanted to do.

Film Career and Notable Works:

Ridley Scott's film career began in 1977 at the age of 40 when he directed his first motion picture, the historical drama The Duellists. The film received praise and recognition at the Cannes Film Festival winning Best First Work. His career work in the diverse genres of Science Fiction, Historical Epic, Crime Drama, and Comedy Drama are known for stunning visual atmosphere, often depicting adventurers in a foreign land adapting to challenging and hostile circumstances. It would be Scott's second feature film in 1979 that remains a testament to how a meticulous detail-oriented approach to filmmaking can elevate a standard story to first-class status and launch a successful ongoing career. He has been nominated for 3 Academy Awards for Best Director. His notable works include:

  • Alien (1979)
  • Blade Runner (1982)
  • Thelma & Louise (1992)
  • Gladiator (2000)
  • Kingdom of Heaven (2005)
  • American Gangster (2007)
  • Prometheus (2012)
  • The Martian (2015)
